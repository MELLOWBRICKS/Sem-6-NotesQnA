Q1. What is the Defect Management Process?

The Defect Management Process (DMP) is a structured approach to identify, document, and resolve defects in software. It helps improve software quality and ensures that defects are handled efficiently.

Phases of Defect Management Process:

  1. Defect Prevention:
    • Implementing best practices to minimize defects.
    • Example: Code reviews, static analysis, and proper documentation.
  1. Defect Detection:
    • Identifying defects during testing or reviews.
    • Example: Unit testing, integration testing, system testing.
  1. Defect Reporting:
    • Logging defects in a defect-tracking system.
    • Includes defect ID, description, severity, priority, and steps to reproduce.
  1. Defect Classification & Prioritization:
    • Classifying defects based on severity and priority.
    • Example: Critical, Major, Minor defects.
  1. Defect Resolution:
    • Developers analyze and fix the defect.
    • The fix is tested and verified by testers.
  1. Defect Closure:
    • Ensuring that the defect is resolved and no longer present.
    • Testers verify the fix before closing the defect.
  1. Defect Analysis & Process Improvement:
    • Learning from past defects to improve future processes.
    • Example: Root Cause Analysis (RCA).

Q2. What is SQA? Explain SQA Plan and Activities.

Software Quality Assurance (SQA):

  • A process that ensures software development meets quality standards and customer requirements.
  • SQA focuses on process improvement and defect prevention rather than detection.

SQA Plan:

A document that outlines quality assurance activities for a software project.

Components of an SQA Plan:

  1. Purpose & Objectives: Defines goals of SQA.
  1. Roles & Responsibilities: Assigns QA team roles.
  1. Standards & Guidelines: Lists industry standards like ISO 9000, CMMI.
  1. Review & Audits: Specifies review processes like peer reviews.
  1. Testing Strategies: Defines unit, integration, and system testing.
  1. Tools & Techniques: Specifies tools for defect tracking and testing.

SQA Activities:

  1. Process Definition & Improvement – Establishing quality standards.
  1. Audits & Reviews – Conducting inspections and technical reviews.
  1. Testing & Verification – Ensuring software meets requirements.
  1. Defect Prevention – Implementing coding best practices.
  1. Risk Management – Identifying and mitigating risks.
  1. Training & Documentation – Educating teams on quality practices.

Q3. Explain Formal and Informal Reviews.

Formal Reviews:

  • Conducted using a well-defined process, documentation, and predefined roles.
  • Types of formal reviews:
    1. Inspection – A structured review to find defects.
    1. Technical Review – Experts analyze technical issues in software.
    1. Walkthrough – The author presents work to a group for feedback.

Informal Reviews:

  • Less structured, peer-to-peer discussions to find errors early.
  • No formal documentation or defined roles.
  • Examples:
    • A developer discussing code with a colleague.
    • A tester quickly reviewing a requirement document.

Q4. Enlist different types of defect classification. Draw Defect Management Process. State the working of each phase.

Defect Classification Types:

  1. Severity-Based Classification:
    • Critical: Blocks entire functionality.
    • Major: Affects a key feature.
    • Minor: Does not significantly impact functionality.
  1. Root Cause-Based Classification:
    • Requirement Defects: Incorrect or missing requirements.
    • Design Defects: Poor architectural decisions.
    • Coding Defects: Incorrect logic or syntax errors.
  1. Environment-Based Classification:
    • Hardware Defects: Issues related to hardware compatibility.
    • Software Configuration Defects: Incorrect settings or dependencies.

Q5. Write a short note on Informal Review.

  • An informal review is an unstructured review process where team members examine software artifacts without formal documentation.
  • Purpose: To detect errors early with minimal effort.
  • Examples:
    • Developers sharing code snippets for quick feedback.
    • A business analyst discussing requirement documents informally.
  • Benefits:
    1. Saves time and effort.
    1. Encourages open discussion.
    1. Identifies defects early.

Q6. Elaborate the concept of Software Metrics? Describe Product and Process Metrics with suitable examples.

Concept of Software Metrics:

  • Software Metrics are quantitative measurements used to assess software quality and efficiency.
  • Types: Process Metrics, Product Metrics, Project Metrics.

Product Metrics (Measure Software Quality):

  1. Size Metrics: Lines of Code (LOC), Function Points.
  1. Complexity Metrics: Cyclomatic Complexity (measures decision paths).
  1. Performance Metrics: Response time, Throughput.

Process Metrics (Measure Development Process Efficiency):

  1. Defect Density: Number of defects per module.
  1. Test Coverage: Percentage of code covered by tests.
  1. Mean Time to Failure (MTTF): Average time before software fails.

Q7. Give Principles of Software Testing.

  1. Testing shows the presence of defects, not their absence.
  1. Exhaustive testing is impossible – Testing all cases is impractical.
  1. Early testing is beneficial – Detecting bugs early reduces cost.
  1. Defect clustering – A few modules contain most defects.
  1. Pesticide paradox – Repeating the same tests loses effectiveness.
  1. Testing depends on context – Different projects need different testing approaches.
  1. Absence-of-errors fallacy – A defect-free system may still fail if requirements are wrong.

Q8. Write a short note on Formal Technical Review (FTR).

  • Formal Technical Review (FTR) is a structured review process to evaluate software artifacts for defects.
  • Conducted by a team of experts with predefined roles.
  • Types of Formal Technical Reviews:
    1. Inspection – Detects errors and ensures adherence to standards.
    1. Walkthrough – Author presents the document to the team.
    1. Technical Review – Experts examine the software’s technical aspects.
  • Steps of FTR:
    1. Planning – Define objectives and participants.
    1. Preparation – Reviewers examine artifacts beforehand.
    1. Review Meeting – Discuss findings and document defects.
    1. Rework – Developer fixes identified defects.
    1. Follow-Up – Ensure corrections are implemented.
