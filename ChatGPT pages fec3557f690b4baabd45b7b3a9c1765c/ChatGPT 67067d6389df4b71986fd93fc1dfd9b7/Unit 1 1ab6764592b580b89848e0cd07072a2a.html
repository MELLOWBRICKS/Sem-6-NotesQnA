Q1. Give the concept of Software Review with its steps.

Concept of Software Review:

  • A Software Review is a systematic examination of a software product to find and resolve issues early in the development lifecycle.
  • It is conducted to ensure that the software meets the required standards and specifications.
  • Software reviews help improve software quality by identifying defects and improving development efficiency.
  • It can be performed at different stages of development, such as requirements, design, code, and test cases.
  • The key participants in a software review include developers, testers, and stakeholders.
  • Common types of software reviews include peer reviews, walkthroughs, and inspections.

Steps of Software Review:

  1. Planning: Define the purpose, scope, and participants of the review.
  1. Preparation: Reviewers study the software artifacts (requirements, code, etc.).
  1. Review Meeting: Reviewers discuss the findings and identify defects.
  1. Recording Issues: Document issues found during the review.
  1. Rework: The author makes necessary corrections.
  1. Follow-up: Verify that changes were made and assess effectiveness.

Q2. What is Software Quality? Explain Quality Assurance.

Software Quality:

  • Software Quality refers to the degree to which software meets customer requirements, performs efficiently, and is free of defects.
  • It ensures reliability, maintainability, usability, and efficiency.
  • Two Aspects:
    1. Quality of Design: Ensures the software is built according to requirements.
    1. Quality of Conformance: Ensures the final product meets specifications.

Quality Assurance (QA):

  • QA is a systematic process to ensure that software development processes meet predefined quality standards.
  • It prevents defects rather than detecting them.
  • It involves process-oriented activities such as planning, auditing, and process improvement.
  • QA follows industry standards like ISO 9001, CMMI, and Six Sigma.
  • Ensures that best practices and methodologies are followed.
  • Helps improve software reliability, efficiency, and maintainability.

Q6. Explain Software Formal Review.

  • A Formal Review is a structured examination of software artifacts.
  • Conducted using defined procedures and checklists to ensure software quality.
  • Involves roles like moderator, reviewers, and author.
  • Types of Formal Reviews:
    1. Inspection – Focuses on defect detection.
    1. Walkthrough – More informal, used for knowledge sharing.
    1. Technical Review – Conducted by experts to check technical aspects.
  • Steps: Planning → Preparation → Review Meeting → Rework → Follow-up.
  • Ensures software meets industry standards and reduces defects.

Q7. What is Quality? Explain QA and QC.

Quality:

  • Quality means meeting customer expectations and ensuring defect-free software.
  • Involves maintainability, reliability, usability, efficiency, and performance.

Quality Assurance (QA):

  • Ensures processes and methodologies maintain software quality.
  • Prevents defects before they occur.
  • Involves planning, documentation, and auditing.

Quality Control (QC):

  • Focuses on detecting and fixing defects in the final product.
  • Involves testing, inspections, and reviews.
  • Ensures that the product meets quality requirements before delivery.

Q8. What is the nature of errors? Write any four limitations of Manual Testing.

Nature of Errors:

Errors in software refer to flaws, mistakes, or defects that cause unexpected behavior or incorrect results. These errors can occur due to various reasons, such as misinterpretation of requirements, coding mistakes, or environmental factors.

Types of Errors:

  1. Syntax Errors – Occur when programming language rules are violated, leading to compilation failures.
  1. Logical Errors – Mistakes in the logic of the program that produce incorrect outputs.
  1. Runtime Errors – Errors that occur while the program is executing, such as division by zero.
  1. Interface Errors – Errors in communication between software components or external systems.
  1. Performance Errors – Issues related to speed, responsiveness, or excessive resource consumption.
  1. Configuration Errors – Errors due to incorrect system settings or incompatible software/hardware configurations.

Four Limitations of Manual Testing:

  1. Time-Consuming – Since test cases are executed manually, the process is slow compared to automated testing.
  1. Prone to Human Errors – Testers may miss certain defects due to oversight, fatigue, or inconsistency.
  1. Limited Test Coverage – It is difficult to execute all possible test scenarios manually, especially for large applications.
  1. Not Suitable for Large-Scale Testing – Regression and performance testing are difficult to handle manually due to repetitive and complex test cases.
